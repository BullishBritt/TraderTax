Q.01
"How do I file prop firm income?"
Prop firm payouts are typically reported on a 1099-MISC as self-employment income. We structure your entity so you only pay tax on what you keep — not the gross payout.

"Do I qualify for mark-to-market?"
MTM (IRC §475) is one of the most powerful elections for active traders. We assess your frequency, intent, and P&L history to determine eligibility and file the election.

How to File Taxes for Prop Firm Trading (Apex, TopStep, Take Profit, Tradeify, Lucid & More)

Prop firm payouts are taxable income — but they're not taxed the same way as personal trading accounts. Most traders have no idea how their funded account income is classified, which forms apply, or how to reduce what they owe.

  • 01
    Determine How You Were Paid1099-NEC (contractor income) vs. payment processor (still taxable, no form) — each changes how you file.
  • 02
    Track Total Payouts Across All FirmsIf you traded Apex + TopStep + Take Profit, every dollar from every firm adds up on Schedule C.
  • 03
    Deduct Your Trading ExpensesEval fees, platform subscriptions, data feeds, monitors, internet — all potentially deductible against your income.
  • 04
    Consider LLC or S-Corp StructureConsistent prop firm income may warrant entity setup to reduce self-employment tax and create cleaner deductions.
  • 05
    File Schedule C + Self-Employment TaxUnlike capital gains, prop firm income is subject to SE tax (~15.3%) unless properly structured.
